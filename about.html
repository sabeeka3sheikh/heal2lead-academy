My name is Sabeeka

The strong one
who forgot to rest.

For years, I was the one who handled it. The crises. The emotions. The weight of other people's worlds. I managed it all with grace, with composure — and with a quiet exhaustion that no one could see.

I thought I was built this way. That strength meant carrying everything. That love meant putting yourself last. That if I stopped — everything would fall apart.

It took everything I had to finally ask: what is this costing me?

That question changed everything. And it's the same question I now help other women face.

Published Author Certified Coach Enneagram Type 9 Root Cause Diagnostician
Background

Seven years in the corporate world.
One decade understanding why people stay stuck.

The Corporate Years

I built a seven-year career in retail leadership in Saudi Arabia. I was competent, reliable, and deeply committed. I was also slowly erasing myself to meet everyone else's expectations. I didn't have the language for it then. I do now.

The $60,000 Investment

I invested over $60,000 in understanding human psychology, emotional intelligence, and transformation. I trained through some of the world's most rigorous coaching and personal development programs — not to collect certificates, but because I needed to understand what I was experiencing. And I was determined to understand it at the deepest level.

The Book

I wrote and published "Give Up! Oh Please… Shut Up!" — a book about the particular kind of resilience that keeps people from healing. About the stories we tell ourselves to justify staying small, staying exhausted, staying silent. It was my first act of reclaiming my voice publicly.

The Identity Work

My most transformative work has been at the level of identity — understanding who I had been told to be, who I had agreed to become, and who I actually am beneath all of it. This is the work I now hold space for with every woman I work with.

What I believe

The things that guide every conversation

Symptoms are not the problem

Anxiety, people-pleasing, burnout — these are signals, not the root. Real healing requires going beneath the surface to find the original wound or belief driving the pattern.

You were not born this way

The patterns that exhaust you were not your design. They were adaptations to environments that required you to be smaller, quieter, more palatable. They can be unlearned.

Faith is not the problem

Culture tells you that sacrifice and self-erasure are deen. They are not. A deeply grounded spiritual life and radical self-respect are not in conflict — they are designed to coexist.

My Approach

Root cause work.
Not symptom management.

I work as a root cause diagnostician. This means I don't start with what you're doing — I start with what's driving it. Before we talk about habits, boundaries, or behaviour change, we map the belief system underneath it.

My framework — the H.E.A.L. Method — moves through four stages: Honor, Excavate, Align, Lead. It is a structured process that respects both your intelligence and your emotional complexity.

I draw on training in emotional intelligence, identity psychology, the Enneagram system, and somatic awareness. But beyond the frameworks, what I bring to every session is the precision that comes from having lived this — and the clarity that comes from having found the way through.

What you can expect

  • Sessions that go beneath the presenting issue to the driving belief
  • Honest, precise feedback — not what you want to hear, what you need to hear
  • Work that is rooted in both psychological insight and spiritual clarity
  • A container that honours your complexity — not a generic programme
  • A guide who has walked this path, not just studied it
